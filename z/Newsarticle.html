City Unveils New Metro Line Connecting East and West Districts

By Ananya Sharma July 22, 2026 6 min read

After three years of construction, the city's transit authority has opened a new metro line linking the eastern and western districts, cutting commute times by nearly half.

The 18-kilometer line includes 14 stations and is expected to serve over 200,000 commuters daily once fully operational. Officials say the project is part of a broader plan to reduce road congestion and lower emissions across the metropolitan area.

Construction faced repeated delays due to underground utility conflicts and funding shortfalls, but transit authority officials say the final result exceeds initial ridership projections from early planning studies.

This line isn't just about faster commutes. It's about reshaping how people move through the city for the next fifty years. — Transit Authority Spokesperson

The line will operate on a phased rollout, with full service across all 14 stations expected by the end of the year. Fares will match existing metro pricing, with discounted passes available for students and senior citizens.
